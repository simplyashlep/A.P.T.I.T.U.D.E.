Oregon Justice System Teaser

Follow the people, the institutions, and the money at the same time.

A.P.T.I.T.U.D.E. turns Oregon's public justice records into a statewide operating picture. This front page is the 30,000-foot view: where people are held, where they are supervised, how the court system is structured, how prison demographics concentrate, and how corrections dollars are distributed.

The useful story is not just that Oregon imprisons people. It is that the supervised population outside prison is far larger than the prison population, while correctional spending remains heavily concentrated in secure custody. Bias Beacon exists to connect those structural choices to courtroom behavior, county practice, and public accountability.
