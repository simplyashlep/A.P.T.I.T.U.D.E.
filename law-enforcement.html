Law enforcement represents the first contact point in the criminal justice system. Police stops, searches, and arrests disproportionately affect certain populations. This section provides data on police activity across Oregon's agencies, focusing on equity and outcome disparities.

Key Insight

Oregon's STOP data reveals that Black and Native American drivers are stopped at rates significantly above their share of the driving population. These disparities persist after controlling for location and time of day.

Data & Sources

Where This Data Comes From

STOP Oregon STOP Program

Mandatory data collection on all traffic and pedestrian stops by Oregon law enforcement agencies since 2018.

DOJ Oregon Department of Justice

Pattern-and-practice investigations, consent decrees, and civil rights enforcement for law enforcement agencies.

UCR FBI Uniform Crime Reports

Nationally standardized crime and arrest data used for benchmarking Oregon agencies against national trends.

ODPS OR Dept. of Public Safety

State-level coordination for training standards, certification, and professional conduct records for officers.
