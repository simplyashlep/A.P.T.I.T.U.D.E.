Probation · Parole · Revocation · Reentry · Community Outcomes

Community supervision represents the largest component of correctional control in the United States. Probation and parole decisions affect over 100,000 Oregonians. This section examines supervision practices, revocation patterns, and their impact on communities and individuals.

Key Insight

Technical violations — not new crimes — drive the majority of community supervision revocations in Oregon. Missed appointments, failed drug tests, and fee non-payment cycle people back into incarceration at enormous cost.

Supervision & Corrections

Community Corrections by County

Oregon operates a county-based community corrections system. Each county manages probation, parole, and local control supervision with state oversight and funding.

Multnomah County Community Corrections

Portland · Active Cases: ~12,400
Large Metro
38% Revocation Rate
62% Technical Violations
$42M Annual Budget

Oregon's largest community corrections operation. Multnomah County manages probation and parole for the Portland metro area, with significant racial disparities in revocation rates — Black supervisees are revoked at 1.8× the rate of white supervisees.

Washington County Community Corrections

Hillsboro · Active Cases: ~7,800
Large Metro
31% Revocation Rate

Second-largest community corrections operation. Washington County uses a risk-needs-responsivity model for supervision and has implemented alternatives to revocation for technical violations.

Where This Data Comes From

DOC Oregon Dept. of Corrections

Manages state prison system and post-prison supervision. Publishes annual population reports and recidivism studies.

OYA Oregon Youth Authority

Supervises youth offenders in community and custody settings. Provides data on juvenile supervision outcomes.

CJC Criminal Justice Commission

Allocates community corrections funding and sets standards for county supervision programs statewide.

County County CJC Reports

Each county publishes annual community corrections reports with caseload, revocation, and budget data.
